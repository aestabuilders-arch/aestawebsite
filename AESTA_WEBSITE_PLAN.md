## 1. Brand & Positioning

### 1.1 What AESTA is

AESTA is a **design-build firm** — architecture, construction, and project management under one roof. Founded 2010. 100+ completed projects across Pudukkottai district, Aranthangi, Karaikudi, Chennai. Architects and civil engineers with NIT/REC Trichy alumni credentials. Part of the Neram ecosystem.

**Key differentiator:** NIT Trichy-credentialed architects in a market dominated by unqualified local contractors and JustDial listings. Design-build single-point accountability — client doesn't have to coordinate separate architect + contractor + supervisor.

### 1.2 Brand meaning

AESTA has four layered meanings we can draw on: *divinely beautiful*, *star-like* (Latin/classical roots), *sunset/set aside* (Sanskrit), and *auction/staff* (Italian). For brand storytelling, we lead on **"divinely beautiful"** and **"star-like"** — fits an architecture firm that builds sustainable, beautiful homes.

### 1.3 Tagline options (pick one)

1. **"Divinely beautiful. Built to last."** — Poetic, leans on name meaning, bilingual-friendly.
2. **"Design. Build. Belong."** — Three-word rhythm, modern agency feel.
3. **"Homes that feel like home."** — Emotional, resonates with TN family values.
4. **"Sustainable homes. NIT-engineered."** — Credibility-forward, rational buyer.
5. **"உங்கள் கனவு வீடு, எங்கள் பொறுப்பு."** ("Your dream home, our responsibility.") — Tamil-first tagline for local market.

**My recommendation:** Use **#1 in English** and **#5 in Tamil** — they complement each other. You'll have bilingual branding from day one.

### 1.4 Brand voice

- **Confident, not boastful.** ("16 years. 100+ homes. Zero compromises.")
- **Client-centric.** Talk about *their* dream home, not our awards.
- **Bilingual fluency.** Tamil where it matters (homepage hero, testimonials, contact), English everywhere (SEO, specs, forms).
- **Transparent.** Pricing visible, specs visible, process visible.

### 1.5 Visual direction

- **Architecture-firm modern.** Think Studio Mumbai, Morphogenesis, Biome Environmental Solutions — generous whitespace, big photography, restrained type.
- **Warm, earthy palette.** Terracotta, limestone cream, sage green, deep charcoal. Avoids generic "builder blue."
- **Serif + sans pairing.** Playfair Display or Fraunces for headlines (distinctive, architectural), Inter or Manrope for body (clean, readable). Tamil: Noto Sans Tamil or Catamaran.
- **Photography-first.** Site photos are the hero. No stock imagery ever. If a section doesn't have a real photo, we use typography or an illustration — never stock.

### 1.6 Logo direction (to be designed)

Since no logo exists, here's the direction for v1:

- **Wordmark-first.** "AESTA" in a refined serif or semi-serif, with slight custom letterforms (e.g., a subtle star/triangle accent in the "A"). Inspiration: SOM, Foster + Partners, Herzog & de Meuron — architectural wordmarks are almost always clean typography, not pictorial marks.
- **Optional monogram:** A stacked "AE" mark for favicon and social avatar.
- **Tamil lockup:** "ஏஸ்டா" (aesta in Tamil script) available for Tamil-market collateral.
- **Deliverables for v1:** Primary wordmark (horizontal), monogram (square), favicon, one-color version. Can be designed in Figma in a day.

## 2. Competitor Analysis & Market Gap

### 2.1 What dominates the local search results today

From research across Pudukkottai, Karaikudi, and surrounding cities:

1. **Directory listings** (JustDial, Sulekha, IndiaMART, Houzz, Getzinfoz) — take 6–8 of top 10 spots for every construction-related search. They rank because of domain authority, not because they're useful to clients. Clients land here, see 20 generic listings, call 3, get spam calls for months.
2. **Template/free-builder sites** (e.g., Karthick Construction on UENI) — basic, amateurish, no specs, no pricing, no photos, no local SEO optimization.
3. **One or two semi-professional players** — e.g., Raams Constructions (Karaikudi), VSN Construction (Madurai), Sri Ramnath (Chettinad specialist). Decent sites but generic copy and weak on SEO.
4. **No one** is doing: transparent pricing tiers, bilingual content, a cost calculator, local city-specific landing pages, design-build positioning, or modern web design.

### 2.2 The gap = our opportunity

AESTA wins by being the **only** firm in this belt that offers:

1. Published pricing with clear specs (builds trust instantly — clients stop calling 5 contractors just to compare rates).
2. Bilingual Tamil + English content (huge trust signal in this market).
3. Dedicated landing pages for every major city (Pudukkottai, Karaikudi, Aranthangi, Thirumayam, Keeranur, Ponnamaravathy, Viralimalai, Alangudi, Illuppur, Gandarvakottai, Thirupathur, Avudaiyarkoil, Trichy, Thanjavur).
4. Real project photos (most competitors have 2–3 stock images or none).
5. NIT Trichy credential — rare and powerful in this market.
6. A fast, beautiful mobile-first site — competitors are slow, ugly, or both.

### 2.3 SEO beat strategy

We will **not** try to outrank JustDial — that's a losing battle. We'll rank alongside them and win on click-through rate and conversion:

- JustDial gives clients 20 numbers to call. We give one firm that will answer every question before they even call.
- We target **long-tail, intent-rich queries** where JustDial's generic pages are weak: "G+1 house construction cost Pudukkottai," "Chettinad style home architect Karaikudi," "best design build firm Aranthangi," "sustainable house construction NIT architect Tamil Nadu."

## 5. Pricing Tiers (The Spec Sheet)

Based on 2026 Tamil Nadu market research, here are the proposed tiers. **You must validate these against what you actually deliver and can profitably price.** I've stayed close to market rates — you can go slightly below to win on price (aggressive) or slightly above to position as premium (what I'd recommend given NIT credential).

### 5.1 Pricing structure (proposed — validate and adjust)

| Tier | Price per sqft | Positioning | Target client |
|------|----------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Economy** | ₹1,999 – ₹2,199 | Entry-level, quality basics | First-time builders, rental property owners |
| **Standard** | ₹2,299 – ₹2,599 | Best value, what most clients pick | Middle-class families, 3BHK homes |
| **Premium** | ₹2,699 – ₹2,999 | Design-forward, better materials | Upper-middle, professionals, NRIs |
| **Luxury** | ₹3,299+ | Bespoke, architect-led, top finishes | Villa, large bungalow, commercial |

**Note on competitive positioning:** Chennai competitors start at ₹1,999 (Deejos), ₹2,100 (Bluemoon), and go up to ₹3,500+ for premium. In Pudukkottai/Karaikudi, rates are typically ₹200–400 lower than Chennai due to lower labor/material costs. So actual Pudukkottai market range: **₹1,800–₹3,200**. We position at the quality-conscious end of the local market while staying accessible.

### 5.3 What gets published vs. kept for quote conversation

**Published publicly on pricing page:** Full tier comparison table (all 25 line items × 4 tiers). This builds enormous trust and differentiates us from every competitor.

**Not published (kept for quote stage):** Profit margins, subcontractor rates, material procurement details, detailed project schedules.

### 5.4 Quote request flow (v1)

1. Client clicks "Get Quote" on any page → lands on `/quote` form
2. Form collects: Name, phone, email (optional), city, plot size (sqft), built-up area planned, floors (G / G+1 / G+2), tier interest (E/S/P/L/Unsure), timeline, specific requirements text area
3. On submit → Supabase writes lead + sends you a WhatsApp alert (via WhatsApp Business API you've already set up) + sends client a WhatsApp confirmation with a brochure link
4. You (or Tamil) follow up manually within 24h to give detailed quote

**Phase 2:** Replace step 4 with an instant PDF quote generator that pulls tier pricing × sqft + shows a range.

## 6. Services List (Final)

Eight service pages for v1. You asked me to help define these — here's what makes sense for a design-build firm in this market:

1. **Residential Construction** — Individual houses, duplexes, bungalows. Core bread-and-butter.
2. **Commercial Construction** — Shops, offices, small commercial complexes. You've done Srinivasan commercial complex — proof point.
3. **Architectural Design** — Standalone design service (2D plans, 3D elevations, structural drawings) for clients who want to use their own contractor. Upsell path to full construction.
4. **Interior Design** — Furnishing, modular kitchens, wardrobes, false ceiling. Natural extension of construction.
5. **Renovation & Extension** — Old house → modern home. You've done Onnangudi renovation — proof point.
6. **Project Management / Site Supervision** — For clients building themselves who need a professional to manage subcontractors. Lower-ticket entry service.
7. **3D Visualization** — Pre-construction walkthroughs for clients who want to see before they commit. Low-cost service, high-trust-building.
8. **Turnkey Homes** — Land-to-keys complete package including DTCP approvals, design, construction, interiors. Premium offering.

**Pricing/charging model — to be finalized:**

| Service | Typical charging method |
|---------|------------------------|
| Residential construction | Per sqft (tier-based) |

**You need to validate these rates against your actual cost structure.** This is a starting framework.

## 7. Technical Architecture

### 7.1 Stack summary

- **Frontend:** Next.js 14 (App Router), TypeScript, Tailwind CSS
- **UI components:** Headless UI or shadcn/ui (same pattern as Nexus avoids MUI bloat)
- **Database:** Supabase (PostgreSQL + Auth + Storage)
- **Hosting:** Vercel (free tier — one more project fits since you've optimized Neram build minutes)

- **Forms:** Server actions → Supabase → WhatsApp Business API webhook

- **Analytics:** Vercel Analytics (free) + Google Search Console + Google Analytics 4
- **Admin panel:** Supabase Auth (magic link or phone OTP) + simple CRUD dashboard in same Next.js app, mounted at /admin with middleware protection
